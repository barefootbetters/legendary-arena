# PAR Artifact Integrity & Hashing

> **Authority:** Explanatory document only. The authoritative contract for hash
> computation, validation, versioning, and index enforcement lives in
> `WP-050: PAR Artifact Storage & Indexing`.

Legendary Arena treats PAR values as **immutable reference artifacts**, not
temporary metrics.

Once published, a PAR value defines the difficulty baseline for competitive
play. Players trust that baseline to remain stable over time.

To preserve that trust, every PAR artifact is **cryptographically hashed**.

---

## What Is a PAR Artifact?

A PAR artifact is the canonical record produced by simulation for a single
scenario (scheme, mastermind, villain groups, player count).

It includes:
- The PAR value
- Distribution statistics from simulation
- Simulation provenance (AI policy tier, seed set hash)
- Scoring version metadata

PAR artifacts are published **before** players choose heroes and **before**
leaderboard play is allowed.

---

## Why Hashing Is Used

Hashing provides **tamper-evident immutability**.

Each PAR artifact:
- Contains a self-hash of its contents (SHA-256, excluding the hash field itself)
- Is also cross-verified by the version index file

This ensures that:
- Accidental edits are detected immediately
- Bad merges cannot silently change difficulty baselines
- Storage backends (filesystem, Git, object storage) remain safe
- Historical PAR values can never be altered retroactively

If any hash mismatch is detected, PAR publication halts.

Hashing is intentionally storage-agnostic: once artifacts are validated, they
may be hosted on local filesystems, Git, object storage, or CDNs without
changing the trust model.

---

## What Hashing Does Not Do

Hashing is **not** about secrecy, encryption, or DRM.

- PAR data is transparent and auditable
- Hashes exist only to protect integrity
- No gameplay logic depends on hashes at runtime

Hashing enforces **trust**, not control.

PAR artifacts are not state. They are precedent.
