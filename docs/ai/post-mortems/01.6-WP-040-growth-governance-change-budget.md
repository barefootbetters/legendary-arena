## Section 9 — Lessons Learned

### 9.1 P6-53 authority-surface enumeration is load-bearing

The v1 pre-flight caught the absent-authorities drift class for the second time in the Phase 7 trio (WP-039 had the same class). In both cases, the v1 WP's Context (Read First) block was incomplete, and the authoring pass did not re-check the landed surfaces before proposing new ones. For WP-039, the missing surfaces were `INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md` and `ops.types.ts`; for WP-040, the missing surfaces expanded to eight documents and one directory. P6-53 (landed in 01.4 at Commit A0a `a6be850`) codifies the discipline: every WP that touches an existing authority must enumerate that authority in Context (Read First) with an **AUTHORITATIVE** caps-tag, and the executing session must re-read each enumerated surface before writing a single line of content.

### 9.2 P6-51 DECISIONS.md placement is resolved at pre-flight, not at execution

The three-entry rationale requirement under WP-040 §DoD could have been satisfied by long-form DECISIONS.md entries restating the prose, or by short back-pointer entries citing the produced doc's sections. The v1 pre-flight identified this as PS-2 and locked form (2) at pre-flight rather than leaving the choice to the executing session. The lesson: ambiguity about where rationale prose lives must be resolved before session open; the executing session should never discover at Commit-B authoring time that form (2) is the correct choice. The WP-035 precedent is now the canonical pattern; form (1) — long-form DECISIONS.md entries restating prose — is now an anti-pattern for governance documents with multiple rationale surfaces.

### 9.3 P6-52 paraphrase discipline is a pre-flight, not a mid-execution, concern

P6-52 (landed in 01.4 at Commit A0a `a6be850`) codifies the grep-vs-prose discipline: a verification-step grep does not distinguish advocacy from opposition; meta-prose arguing against a forbidden identifier will trip the grep identically. The WP-039 post-mortem §10.1 recorded a mid-execution paraphrase fix (`MetricCategory` inside meta-prose). WP-040 applied the discipline proactively at pre-flight v2 by publishing the eleven-entry paraphrase table in the session prompt's §Locked Values before authoring began. Verification Step 5 returned CLEAN on first run. The lesson: proactive paraphrase loading under P6-50 is the correct response to P6-52 class gates — wait-and-paraphrase-when-the-grep-fires is a slower loop and a worse habit.

### 9.4 Three Copilot RISK FIXes, all scope-neutral, all folded into Locked Values

The 01.7 Copilot Check surfaced three Issues (4, 5, 26) at pre-flight v2. None were scope-expansions; all three were authoring-discipline refinements that would have prevented reader-confusion in the produced doc:
